1. KNOWLEDGE AREAS IN COMPUTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

COMPUTER SCIENCE (CS)
- Focuses on the journey from theory and programming to cutting-edge 
  development of computing solutions.
- Provides a foundation that allows graduates to adapt to new technologies.
- Typically requires a strong mathematical and analytical background.
- Main work categories:
    * Designing and building software.
    * Developing effective ways to solve computing problems (e.g., data 
      storage, network transmission, or new security approaches).
    * Devising better ways to use computers for specific challenges like 
      robotics, computer vision, or digital forensics.

INFORMATION SYSTEMS (IS)
- Concerned with information and processes that computer systems provide to 
  help organizations (business, non-profit, or government) achieve goals.
- Professionals bridge the gap between technical and organizational factors 
  to improve overall performance.

COMPUTER ENGINEERING (CE)
- Teaches the design and development of electrical components found in 
  computers and peripheral devices.
- Focuses on application/systems programming, data structures, and networks.

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (IT)
- Prepares students to meet the technology needs of business, government, 
  healthcare, and schools.
- IT professionals select, integrate, install, customize, and maintain 
  hardware and software products to provide a secure environment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CAREERS IN THE COMPUTING INDUSTRY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The industry employs ~10.5 million workers with a 2-3% annual growth rate.

IT DEPARTMENT SPECIALIZATIONS:
- MANAGEMENT: CIO, VP of IT, Chief Security Officer, and Project Leaders.
- DEVELOPMENT & PROGRAMMING: Systems Analysts, Application Programmers, 
  Software Engineers, and Technical Leads.
- TECHNICAL SERVICES: Database Administrators (DBA), Network Security 
  Specialists, Computer Forensics Specialists, and QA Specialists.
- OPERATIONS & TRAINING: Computer Operators, Help Desk Specialists, 
  and Corporate Trainers.

OTHER CAREER PATHS:
- COMPUTER HARDWARE: Design, manufacture, and production of equipment.
- SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT: Producing and supporting software products.
- COMPUTER SERVICE & REPAIR: Maintenance and technical repair services.
- IT CONSULTANT: Providing expertise in areas like system design and 
  network installation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Certifications verify technical knowledge and improve career standing.

- APPLICATION SOFTWARE: Proficiency in programs (e.g., MOS, Adobe ACE).
- OPERATING SYSTEMS: Mastery of systems (e.g., Red Hat, MCSA).
- HARDWARE: Setup and repair of physical components (e.g., CompTIA A+).
- NETWORKING: Planning and troubleshooting networks (e.g., CCNP, Network+).
- SECURITY & FORENSICS: Risk control and evidence analysis (e.g., CISSP).
- DATABASE: Managing systems like Oracle, IBM DB2, or Sybase.
- PROGRAMMING: Training in development (e.g., Sun Certified, MCPD).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI) & MACHINE LEARNING
- AI mimics human intelligence; Machine Learning allows computers to 
  discover patterns from data without explicit programming.

ROBOTIC PROCESS AUTOMATION (RPA)
- Software bots that automate repetitive tasks like transaction processing.

BLOCKCHAIN
- A consensus-driven, secure system where data is permanent and unchangeable.

EDGE COMPUTING
- Processing data near the source to reduce latency compared to the cloud.

VR & AR
- VR creates immersive digital worlds; AR adds digital elements to reality.

CYBER SECURITY
- An ever-emerging field that evolves to defend against new hacking methods.

INTERNET OF THINGS (IoT)
- Everyday items (cars, appliances) connected to the internet to exchange data.
